Abstract

The Monte Carlo Modelis is a series of computer simulations of random actions and processes.The topic of this report is that recording the process of approximating the distribution function of standard normal by the Monte Carlo Method.

Math Equation

The equation for the standard normal distribution \(N(0, 1)\) is

\[Phi(t) = \int_{- \infty}^t \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-y^2 / 2}dy\]

The equation for the Monte Carlo method is

\[\hat\Phi(t) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n I(X_i \le t)\]

where \(X_i\) is a random sample from \(N(0, 1)\), \(I(\cdot)\) is the indicator function. Experiment with the approximation at \(n \in \{10^2, 10^3, 10^4\}\) at \(t \in \{0.0, 0.67, 0.84, 1.28, 1.65, 2.32, 2.58, 3.09, 3.72\}\) to form a table.
